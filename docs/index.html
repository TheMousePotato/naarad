Awaaz IIT Kharagpur

Students of IIT Kharagpur have again made us all proud with their stupendous performance in Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship (QInF) program, 2017. Three out of six winners are from IIT Kharagpur and the other three includes one team each from IIT Bombay, IIIT Hyderabad and IIsc Bangalore. These six winning teams will receive a financial award of INR 10 lakhs (approx. $15,000) each for the academic year 2017-18 and mentorship from Qualcomm engineers. This program recognises, rewards and mentors innovative graduate students across a broad range of technical research.
The winning teams from IIT Kharagpur are:
1) Avisek Lahiri and Abhinav Agarwalla from The Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, recommended by Prof. Prabir Kumar Biswas and Prof. Pabitra Mitra for their proposal on “Domain adversarial networks for bridging cross domain semantic gaps in computer vision”.
2) Sayandeep Saha and Sikhar Patranabis from The Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, recommended by Prof. Debdeep Mukhopadhyay and Prof. Pallab Dasgupta for their proposal on “Automating fault based cryptanalysis in secure embedded applications using machine learning”.
3) Preety Priya and Sucharita Chakraborty from The Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, recommended by Prof. Debarati Sen for their proposal on “Blind joint equalization and detection in presence of non-linearity and synchronization errors for millimeter wave communications”.

Awaaz, IIT Kharagpur congratulates the winners and wishes them all the best for their future endeavours.

WorldQuant Summer Training Program

Based on the success of last year’s Quantitative Research Summer Training Program, we would like to invite applicants for the 2017 program.
Our program offers distinctive hands-on experience in developing mathematical or quantitative models using our web-based stock simulation platform (Websim). With over 1300 registrations, 62 students across various schools in India qualified for the 2016 Summer Training Program to become Research Consultants with WorldQuant.

Our Summer Training Program is designed to offer insight into the quantitative model building space. The virtual training program is a unique learning opportunity within the investment management industry. It is a tailored program that combines virtual training and workshop sessions in developing alphas/trading models.
If you show potential and demonstrate exceptional performance during your time with us, you may be offered a part-time Research Consultant position with us.

Below are a few key highlights of the Summer Training Program:
Ø Regular online training sessions;

Ø Mentoring and guidance from experienced quantitative researchers;

Ø Eligible participants have the potential of being considered for a Research Consultant opportunity at the end of program, which may include aone-time special stipend of up to 1,00,000 INR;

Ø Certificate from WorldQuant.

Participation Requirements:

Hold or working towards Bachelor’s degree or Master’s degree in Engineering, Science, Mathematics, Finance or any other related field that ishighly analytical and quantitative
1st and 2nd year students will be given preference
Have a strong interest in learning about worldwide financial markets
Submit applications to your placement team
Register for a Websim account [ https://websim.worldquant... ] If you have already expressed interest through your institution’s placement office, please make sure you sign up for an account using the same email address. Please fill out your academic profile when applying for a new account.

Space in the program is limited, so selection of candidates for the program will be based on the applicant’s activity in Websim and academic achievement among other factors.

Training Program Completion:
Trainees who attend all scheduled training sessions, complete assignments and build a score on Websim based on the alphas they submit will receive a completion certificate at the end of the training program.
