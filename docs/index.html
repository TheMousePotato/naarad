Anthill Creations aims to build sustainable playscapes for children by using recyclable materials. The idea of Anthill Creations is to create the world where communities come together and active play becomes affordable and accessible to all. It works towards lighter, quicker and cheaper transformation of dead zones into interactive and sustainable playscapes from scrap tyres and recycled material by mobilizing the communities. Recycling non-biodegradable material like tyres, therefore, would not just help safeguard the environment but be cost effective as a building material too.
“Traditionally, mass produced recreational equipment for building a playground would cost a very high price, but since we are using material that is considered trash by some, we have been successful in setting up playgrounds for very cheap cost,” Souradeep had said in an interview.

Thirteen students from the Kharagpur RoboSoccer Students' Group (KRSSG) team represented the institute in the recently concluded RoboCup held in Nagoya (Japan). For qualification, they had submitted a video in the month of February and qualified for the final event.
RoboCup is an annual international robotics competition proposed and founded in 1997 with the aim of promoting robotics and AI research, by offering a publicly appealing, but formidable challenge. This year RoboCup was held in Nagoya, Japan. They participated in two leagues in RoboCup - Small Sized League and Humanoid Simulation League.

A total of twenty-four teams had qualified for the league, out of which only twenty could finally make it to the league. The twenty teams were divided into four groups. From each group, first two teams on the basis of performance were selected for the quarter finals. Unfortunately, they couldn’t make it to the quarters.
In the humanoid simulation league, it was their second participation. They stood 8th among 13 teams. They were able to defeat 5 different teams in one to one matches. They won a total of 7 matches, drew 3 and were in the quarter finals. They missed the chance to go to the semi finals by just one match!
Next year, the RoboCup will be held in Montreal, Canada. We hope for a better performance and even better results in the upcoming RoboCup.

A team of students from IIT Kharagpur, headed by Prof. Aurobinda Routray at the Department of Electrical Engineering, have created a device named Smart Hygiene Monitor (SHM) that helps in reducing the use of water, harmful cleaning agents and even manpower by alerting authorities about the need to clean an area (toilets, eateries, hospitals, parks, airports, railway stations) only when needed.
The Smart Hygiene Monitor (SHM) records levels of ammonia, sulphur dioxide, carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, volatile organic compounds and particulate matter through sensors and sends online alerts to stakeholders when a certain threshold is crossed. It can be installed at any place where cleanliness needs to be monitored at regular intervals. At present, these public facilities are generally cleaned at fixed intervals, irrespective of how dirty they are.
The device has been developed using an embedded platform incorporated with sensors that are connected via the Internet of Things (IoT). “This monitor using sensors detects the level of hygiene in a room and through cloud sends data to the stakeholders,” Prof. Routray said, adding that the setup cost for a 20-sq ft area would be less than Rs 5,000.

According to a recent report by Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) of India for the year ended March 2016, avoidable expenses and irregularities at some of the IITs have cost Rs. 18.03 crores. In this Compliance Audit Observations report, these are some of the few points made about these irregularities:
1) Idle expenditure: Failure on the part of the IIT Roorkee to construct sewer line across NH-58 and STP at Roorkee and Saharanpur campuses resulted in an idle expenditure of Rs. 15.06 crore incurred on the construction of sewer line which could not be utilised even after the lapse of more than four years from the scheduled date of completion.

2) Irregular payment of Service Tax: IIT Roorkee made payment of service tax amounting to Rs. 1.34 crores on the outsourced services, although these services were exempted from payment of such tax. Same goes for IIT Patna with Rs. 8.36 crores.

3) Avoidable expenditure towards penal charges on electricity consumption: IIT Madras had incurred an avoidable expenditure of Rs. 1.05 crores towards penalty for exceeding contracted demand during April 2013 to March 2016 due to non-review and enhancement of sanctioned demand.

4) Irregular waiver of overpayment: The Board of Governors of IIT Jodhpur irregularly waived recovery of excess payment amounting to Rs. 59.38 lakhs made to its faculty, which is now under recovery at the instance of Audit.

5) Irregular reimbursement of LTC (Leave Travel Concession) claims by Central Autonomous Bodies: Central Autonomous Bodies irregularly reimbursed air fares of Rs. 6.90 crores during 2012-16 against the air tickets purchased by their employees from unauthorised agents in violation of the MoF guidelines for availing Leave Travel Concession. IIT Kharagpur, despite delayed submission of claims, did not recover LTC advance of Rs. 1.14 crore of which Rs. 19.85 lakh was to be forfeited. Cross verification of claims with the Airlines also revealed that the air fares reimbursed by the IIT Kharagpur and IIT Bombay were inflated by Rs. 18.56 lakhs (11.21 lakhs for IIT Kharagpur, 7.35 lakhs for IIT Bombay).
